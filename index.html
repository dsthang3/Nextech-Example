Colossal Costs

The economic costs of air pollution from fossil fuels are estimated at US$2.9 trillion in 2018, or 3.3% of global GDP, far exceeding the likely costs of rapid reductions in fossil fuel use. An estimated 4.5 million people died in 2018 due to exposure to air pollution from fossil fuels. - Centre for Research on Energy and Clean Air

How We Can Help

Increasing efficiency in energy use and adopting environmentally benign fuel sources can significantly reduce environmental impact. Reducing water and chemical inputs in various processes, along with the adoption of less harmful pesticides or cultivation of pest-resistant crop strains, also contributes to sustainability. Protecting sensitive areas is essential for preserving biodiversity. Modifying production processes to generate less waste, and using non-toxic or less toxic chemicals for cleaning and maintenance, further supports environmental goals. Implementing water and energy conservation practices, such as reusing materials like drums and pallets and switching to reusable water bottles, helps minimize waste. Automatically turning off lights when not in use, repairing leaky faucets and hoses, and switching to 'green' cleaners are additional steps to enhance sustainability and reduce resource consumption.
